\begin{abstract}
We validate the $k$-Nearest Neighbor Landy--Szalay estimator for the two-point correlation function $\xi(r)$ using the CoxMock suite: an isotropic line-point Cox process with analytically known $\xi(r)$.  Over $N_{\rm mock}$ realizations we verify (i)~unbiasedness of the estimator to within the statistical precision, (ii)~consistency of the scatter with Poisson expectations, and (iii)~correct recovery of the kNN-CDF shapes.  This document is a standalone validation appendix intended for later incorporation into the main paper.
\end{abstract}

\section{The CoxMock Generator}

We use an isotropic line-point Cox process following the Euclid 2PCF-GC validation suite (de la Torre et al.\ 2025).  $N_L$ lines of length $\ell$ are placed at random positions and orientations in a periodic cube of side $L$.  $N_p$ points are scattered uniformly on randomly chosen lines.  Points and line segments that extend beyond the box boundaries are mirrored (periodic boundary conditions).

The analytic two-point correlation function for this process is:
\begin{equation}
  \xi(r) = \frac{m(m-1)\,V}{N_p^2\,\ell}\;\frac{1}{r}\;\Bigl(1 - \frac{r}{\ell}\Bigr)\,,\qquad r < \ell\,,
  \label{eq:xi_cox}
\end{equation}
where $m = N_p/N_L$ is the mean number of points per line, $V = L^3$ is the box volume, and $\xi(r) = 0$ for $r \geq \ell$.  This is a damped $r^{-1}$ profile; the pair count $\propto r^2\,\xi(r) \propto r\,(1 - r/\ell)$ peaks at $r = \ell/2$.

\paragraph{Parameters.}  For the initial validation we use: $L = 1000\,h^{-1}\,\mathrm{Mpc}$, $N_L = 10{,}000$ lines, $\ell = 400\,h^{-1}\,\mathrm{Mpc}$, $N_p = 100{,}000$ points, giving $\bar{n} = 10^{-4}\,h^3\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-3}$ and $m = 10$ points per line.

\section{Estimator}

The primary estimator is Landy--Szalay in the Hamilton form, with pair-count densities reconstructed from the $k$NN distance distributions:
\begin{equation}
  1 + \hat{\xi}(r) = \frac{\mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{DD}}(r)\cdot\mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{RR}}(r)}{[\mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{DR}}(r)]^2}\,,
  \label{eq:ls_knn}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{D}^{XY}(r) = \sum_{k=1}^{k_{\max}} \mathrm{pdf}^{XY}_k(r)$ is the summed pair-count density from the $k$NN queries of catalog $X$ against catalog $Y$.

For a uniform periodic box, $\mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{RR}}(r)$ is the uniform density $\bar{n}$ (constant), and Eq.~\eqref{eq:ls_knn} reduces to Davis--Peebles: $1 + \hat{\xi}(r) = \mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{DD}}(r)/\mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{DR}}(r)$.  We use this simplified form for the periodic-box validation.

\subsection{Test 2: kNN-CDF consistency}

For the random catalog (Poisson in a periodic box), the $k$NN-CDFs should match the Erlang distribution exactly:
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{CDF}^{\mathrm{RR}}_k(r) = 1 - e^{-\bar{n}\,V(r)}\sum_{j=0}^{k-1}\frac{[\bar{n}\,V(r)]^j}{j!}\,,\qquad V(r) = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\,.
\end{equation}
